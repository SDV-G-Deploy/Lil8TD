# Lil8TD Visual Research Pack V1 — Cozy RTS Miniatures x Dandadan Energy

Status: direction study, pre-production art brief input.

Purpose: define how Lil8TD can borrow **energy, contrast, and accent logic** from a Dandadan-like visual language while keeping the core game readable as a warm old-school RTS/TD board.

---

## 1. Executive summary

**Recommendation:**
Do **not** make Lil8TD "look like Dandadan" literally.
Instead, build Lil8TD around **Cozy RTS Miniatures** as the base style, then layer in a **Dandadan-inspired accent system** for:

- magical color spikes,
- stronger silhouette drama,
- more kinetic enemy/tower identity,
- sharper mood contrast during combat moments.

In short:

**Base = AoE2-adjacent cozy handcrafted battlefield**  
**Accent = Dandadan-like high-contrast supernatural energy**

That combination is much more promising than either source alone.

---

## 2. Why Dandadan is useful here

From research, Dandadan is strong not because of one static palette, but because of a few deeper traits:

1. **High contrast with intentional restraint**
   - calm base scenes
   - explosive supernatural accent zones
   - colors used as a language, not decoration

2. **Strong silhouette personality**
   - characters read fast by posture and outline
   - even absurd or exaggerated designs stay memorable

3. **Kinetic energy**
   - motion, streaks, directional emphasis, visual impulse
   - action feels alive even before full detail is parsed

4. **Mood via color families**
   - different forces/entities can feel different through distinct color families
   - warm vs cold contrasts are very legible

5. **Comedy + menace coexistence**
   - charming and strange at once
   - this is relevant because Lil8TD can benefit from being lovable without becoming soft or bland

---

## 3. Why Dandadan cannot be the full base style

Lil8TD is a top-down TD/RTS-like board game view.
That creates constraints Dandadan does not have:

- gameplay must read instantly at zoomed-out size
- terrain must stay calm
- path must remain the first read
- towers and enemies must read from silhouette before effect color
- the whole board must survive phone-screen screenshots

So if we apply Dandadan too literally, we risk:

- visual chaos,
- too much dramatic contrast in the background,
- anime-poster energy where the board needs structural clarity,
- loss of old-school RTS dignity,
- a "stylish but annoying to play" result.

Therefore:

**Dandadan is a spice layer, not the plate.**

---

## 4. Core direction recommendation

## Chosen base direction

### Cozy RTS Miniatures

Lil8TD should still be:

- warm medieval-fantasy,
- handcrafted battlefield,
- miniature hero towers,
- tiny readable raiders/creatures,
- wood / brass / parchment UI,
- calm terrain with strong gameplay hierarchy.

This remains the correct north star.

## Added secondary influence

### Dandadan-style kinetic accent logic

Borrow only these aspects:

- bolder role contrast,
- stronger supernatural color coding,
- more memorable role silhouettes,
- more dynamic combat accent moments,
- more expressive micro-motion and attack feel.

### 5.1 Base palette families for Lil8TD

Use calm grounded families for the board itself:

- moss green
- olive green
- dusty earth brown
- muted stone gray-brown
- dark wood
- bronze/brass
- parchment cream

These should carry:

- grass/build tiles
- path body
- tower bases
- main UI slabs

### 5.2 Dandadan-derived accent palette families

Use the attached palette for spikes and identity:

#### Warm danger / aggression
- `crimson_red`
- `warm_red`
- `deep_wine`

Use for:
- enemy banners/details
- hostile magic accents
- hit flashes
- burst tower core accents
- danger state UI markers

#### Mystical / psychic / frost separation
- `dark_teal`
- `forest_teal`
- `cyan_fog`
- `pale_mist`

Use for:
- frost tower identity
- magical slow effects
- psychic/eldritch future enemy families
- atmospheric spell accents

#### Noble ornament / premium trim
- `gold_thread`
- `amber_highlight`

Use for:
- tower trims
- UI edge highlights
- premium button/selection accents
- rare path ornaments and insignia

#### Deep shadow system
- `deep_black`
- `charcoal_shadow`
- `dark_plum_shadow`

Use for:
- richer sprite shadows
- dramatic silhouette read
- combat readability
- supernatural depth accents

### 5.3 Palette rule

**Base tiles should be quieter than units.**  
**Accent colors should belong mostly to roles, powers, and combat punctuation.**

That is the safest way to gain Dandadan energy without wrecking RTS readability.

## 6. Terrain doctrine under this direction

Terrain should remain mostly **Cozy RTS Miniatures**, not Dandadan.

### Terrain goals
- calm
- handcrafted
- layered but quiet
- low-noise
- mobile-readable

### Terrain should borrow from Dandadan only in tiny ways
Possible acceptable uses:
- deeper shadow separation with plum/charcoal notes
- rare teal fog/magic contamination on special maps later
- slightly stronger warm/cool contrast between safe ground and magical danger zones

### Terrain should NOT become
- neon
- highly saturated
- dramatic every tile
- painterly-chaotic
- anime background first, gameplay second

Conclusion:

**Dandadan energy belongs around gameplay beats, not in the main moss carpet.**

---

## 7. Path doctrine under this direction

Path must still read first.

Best path approach:
- warm earthy spine
- slightly darker shoulders
- worn highlight bands
- occasional crafted ornaments
- maybe rare tiny red/gold roadside details as storytelling, not decoration spam

If Dandadan influence is used here, it should be subtle:
- stronger warm-vs-cool separation around active combat
- occasional supernatural tint only in advanced/fantasy map variants

Not for base V2:
- high-contrast anime patterns on the road
- strong hue shifts every few tiles
- over-stylized dramatic lane gradients

---

## 8. Tower doctrine under this direction

This is where Dandadan influence helps the most.

### Desired result
Towers should become:
- more heroic,
- more memorable,
- more expressive,
- more silhouette-driven.

### Principle
Take the current cozy miniature tower idea and add:
- stronger top-shape drama,
- clearer role energy,
- more distinct magical or mechanical identity.

### By tower
#### Arrow Tower
Base identity:
- reliable physical defense piece

Dandadan influence:
- sharper silhouette
- stronger pennant / bow arc / directional tension
- slightly bolder red-gold trim possible

#### Burst Tower
Base identity:
- hot, explosive, alchemical, dangerous

Dandadan influence:
- ember-core glow
- richer crimson/warm-red center
- more dramatic cannon mouth or bomb profile

#### Frost Tower
Base identity:
- eerie, magical, elegant control piece

Dandadan influence:
- colder teal/cyan aura
- more supernatural crystal crown
- stronger shape contrast between calm body and dangerous tip

### Tower rule
**Dandadan should make towers more iconic, not more noisy.**

---

## 9. Enemy doctrine under this direction

This is the second strongest place for Dandadan influence.

### Desired result
Enemies should stop reading as tokens and start reading as:
- tiny raiders,
- weird creatures,
- aggressive little personalities.

### What to borrow
- stronger posture
- stronger directional energy
- bolder outline language
- clearer role-specific color families

### By enemy
#### Grunt
- grounded, sturdy, readable infantry mass
- can hold warmer earthy-red accents
- more soldier/raider than blob

#### Runner
- more kinetic, fox-like, eerie, slightly mischievous
- can benefit from teal/red contrast streaks
- should look fast even when frozen in silhouette

#### Brute
- wider, heavier, stranger
- silhouette first, detail second
- can hold darker plum shadow notes to feel more ominous

### Enemy rule
Use Dandadan for:
- **energy and personality**
not for:
- random weirdness without role clarity.

---

## 10. UI doctrine under this direction

UI should remain mostly **LW2B / Cozy RTS** in structure.

That means:
- wood
- bronze
- parchment
- carved edges
- stable command layout

Where Dandadan can help:
- better accent highlights
- stronger danger/selection states
- more flavorful magical role icons
- richer shadow contrast
- more stylized glyphs for special states

UI should NOT become:
- anime-tech
- neon supernatural all the time
- poster-like
- overillustrated

Conclusion:

**UI chassis = LW2B logic**  
**UI accents = Dandadan mood spikes**

---

## 11. Motion / FX doctrine

This is the strongest system-level place to borrow Dandadan energy.

### Good Dandadan-derived moves
- stronger attack streaks
- better directional impulse in projectiles
- more stylized hit punctuation
- teal or crimson energy edges by role
- slightly more elastic motion feel on tiny units

### Safe implementation idea
- keep deterministic sim untouched
- all changes render-side only
- use color trails and impact timing to create emotional energy

### Ideal split
- normal movement = modest and readable
- attack / hit / special effect = brief Dandadan-like spike of energy

This gives the game more life without turning the board into chaos.

## 12. Three possible style branches

## Branch A — Cozy RTS Miniatures + Dandadan Accents (**recommended**)

### Ratio
- 75% cozy RTS base
- 25% Dandadan accent energy

### Best for
- Lil8TD core identity
- mobile readability
- future growth
- avoiding cheapness

### Result
Warm handcrafted battlefield with sharp magical combat accents and more characterful units.

---

## Branch B — Storybook Fairytale x Dandadan

### Ratio
- 55% storybook softness
- 45% Dandadan energy

### Best for
- stronger charm
- more emotional/fantasy push

### Risk
Can slide into soft/mobile/fairytale over-sweetness.

---

## Branch C — Dark Occult Miniatures

### Ratio
- 50% cozy miniatures
- 50% dark supernatural drama

### Best for
- more menace
- stronger weirdness
- elite/night maps later

### Risk
Too gloomy for base identity.
Better as a variant biome/theme, not the mainline style.

---

## 13. Final recommendation

For **Sprite Set V2**, choose:

# Branch A — Cozy RTS Miniatures + Dandadan Accents

This is the most promising and stable direction.

It lets Lil8TD be:
- warm,
- crafted,
- old-school,
- readable,
- and still more memorable and stylish than a plain RTS tribute.

## 15. Anti-patterns

Do not:
- repaint the whole terrain with crimson/teal drama
- make every object hyper-contrasty at once
- use anime energy where calm board hierarchy is needed
- confuse "stylish" with "busy"
- replace RTS dignity with pure cartoon chaos
- let accent colors take over the neutral board structure
