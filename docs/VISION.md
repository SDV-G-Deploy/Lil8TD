# Lil8TD Vision

## Project identity

Lil8TD is a compact competitive tower defense project with:
- top-down camera
- old school pixel-art presentation
- deterministic simulation mindset
- solo play versus AI bots
- online 1v1 matches

The goal is not to clone LW2B.
The goal is to build a new genre-fit game while reusing the best engineering and design lessons from LW2B and MultiWebCore.

## Product intent

The project should aim for:
- readable matches
- short-to-medium session length
- strong replayability through builds, timing, lane pressure, and adaptation
- low-chaos multiplayer behavior
- clean path from local/offline testing to real online matches

## Experience goals

### Visual feel
- old school pixel style
- readable silhouettes
- low visual noise
- strong lane/tile readability
- clear attack ranges and threat states

### Gameplay feel
- immediate readability
- meaningful placement decisions
- timing windows that matter
- pressure without requiring RTS-grade micro
- comeback room without random rubber-banding

### Competitive feel
- online 1v1 should reward planning, adaptation, and timing
- matches should be decided more by decisions than by click speed
- the networking layer should feel stable and boring in the best way

## Design stance

Lil8TD should be:
- systemically clean
- compact in scope
- easy to reason about
- strong in foundations before content explosion

Lil8TD should not be:
- an overgrown RTS in disguise
- dependent on peer-to-peer fragility
- built around hidden AI cheats
- overloaded with genre-incompatible complexity in MVP
